It’s time to launch! It’s been a lot of work over the past month and a half or so, and a lot of changes have transpired specifically over the past few days. First and foremost, we have migrated servers. We are now hosted on a dedicated machine in a datacenter in Canada with an immaculate connection, more RAM than we know what to do with, and a processor that should be able to laugh off the worst we throw at it. This is the big one, and our hosting costs have gone up a bit as a result, but I’m confident it’ll be worth it and I’m willing to bankroll it for a while to prove the server’s vision.

Secondly, I’ve started the Map Image project for the server, which is an initiative I want to pursue to use maps as in-game ‘posters’ to help players navigate the server and to add a lot of flavor and character to the worlds. All of the worlds on the server now have map images to go along with them so that the portals that lead to them from The Crossroads are clearly marked. Here’s some examples:

 

I typically view pixel art as a blight on Minecraft, but in this particular instance it’s proving to be incredibly useful. Now we can do things like make in-universe posters for Rapture, or post helpful arrows to guide players around! I’m hoping I get some good artists in the community to help me with this.

Now, I know that Minecraft 1.5 just came out. Believe me, nobody is more interested in getting patched up and playing with the new Redstone toys than I am. However, for the sake of my sanity and the server’s stability, we’re going to stay nice and cozy on Minecraft 1.4.7 for a while – at least until I see that all of the core plugins that are required to keep the server afloat have patched without issue.

With all that said, things are looking fairly positive, and I’m hoping to start inviting the community at large to come play on Crafti.ca shortly! If you’re looking for somewhere new, consider giving the server a shot. I think you’ll be pleasantly surprised.

]]> /texture-pack-now-available-for-download/feed/ 0 Crafti.ca is nearing launch! /crafti-ca-is-nearing-launch/ /crafti-ca-is-nearing-launch/#comments Mon, 18 Feb 2013 00:54:13 +0000 /?p=550 We’ve been hard at work setting up the foundation of Crafti.ca – finishing the Crossroads, tweaking plugins and permissions, and ensuring that all the bells and whistles we’re implementing work together as seamlessly as possible.

Currently, we’ve managed to set the donations page up to interface with the server directly, so any promotions or perks associated with donating should take place almost immediately. We’ve also set up a handy little monthly donation bar on the right-hand side of the site, so you can see just how your contributions help the server meet its goals! We’re currently hosted via the fantastic guys over at EnforcerCraft, who offer both dirt-cheap hosting and powerful options for us to hopefully grow into down the road.

A copy of the code powering Minotar has also been implemented on the site, and it follows a similar URL structure. We’ve successfully integrated this into both the main site and our forums, so any time an avatar is required it should automatically default to your minecraft skin, provided the username you use is the same as your in-game Minecraft name. Similarly, the server status checks have now successfully been implemented throughout the whole site, and should provide at-a-glance information fairly reliably in the top right of every page in the header.

We’ve also been looking at pushing the same sort of permissions for donators on the forum in the form of fancy forum badges, but thusfar we have been unsuccessful in actually making the server and phpBB communicate effectively. There are a few bukkit plugins that already exist to solve this problem, but all of them are designed to pull information and permissions from the forums to the server, and not to push them the other way around. Also complicating this task is the fact that phpBB forums are very security-minded, and as a result are incredibly paranoid about accepting any inbound data from outside sources.

While Ein furiously tries to finish the back-end of things and rewrite site content, those of you who have gotten early access are encouraged to pursue the job of turning the Crossroads into something more resembling an adventure map for our future exploration-minded guests to enjoy. At this point, we anticipate launch is only a few days away – stay tuned!
